## 1. Scope & method

This roadmap proposes a milestone sequence for agents-config covering the path from "current state" to a working, stable MVP, plus immediate post-MVP capabilities. **Audience:** Scott (primary, dogfooding the harness daily); broader AI-assisted devs (secondary, readiness markers per milestone).

**What this doc is:** a sequence of user-capability-framed milestones, each with mechanically/behaviorally verifiable Definition of Done, drawn from the open + in-progress backlog (107 + 10 = 117 strategic beads; ~78 substantive after stripping merge-gate stubs and molecule step-beads). Recommendations include cuts, research-spike resolutions, deferrals, and structural changes (parents, dependencies, labels). **What this doc is not:** an estimate of wall-clock time; an authorization to begin beadification (Phase 5b is gated on Scott's explicit go); a fixed contract — the roadmap is a planning artifact, not a binding plan.

**Method:** three parallel research forks (Inventory & Cluster — Fork-A; Dependency Critique — Fork-B; Vision Alignment — Fork-C), Scott checkpoint, opus[1m] synthesis. The full pipeline lives in the design spec.

**How to update:** when this doc drifts from the actual roadmap intent, re-run the design's pipeline against a fresh bead snapshot; do NOT patch this doc in-place beyond minor corrections.

## 2. Vision recap

The five load-bearing commitments from `AGENTS.md`'s Vision & Mission section translate into roadmap horizon as follows: **Commitment 1** (frontload human creativity via rigorous brainstorming) is served by a brainstorm-readiness epic that makes spec quality a mechanical phase rather than cultural aspiration. **Commitment 2** (make AI good at saying "no, not ready") becomes a hard-gate that bounces under-specified beads when the `implementation-ready` label is being added (`bd label add <id> implementation-ready`) — currently entirely unbuilt. **Commitments 3 + 4** (substitute adversarial cross-model review; mechanical evidence at completion) are partially served by existing RALF / completion-gate / verify-checklist machinery; the remaining work is the auto-merge tiering policy that lets RALF substitute for human review on appropriate PR classes. **Commitment 5** (persist context — beads, memories, formulas — so work survives compaction, handoff, and overnight) is the longest-running work item: it requires the worker fleet (7bk family), HEP (7bk.13/7bk.20), and the production shell driver (7bk.11). The roadmap horizon is the four milestones that take 85/5/10 from aspiration to mechanical reality on Scott's daily dogfooding.

## 5. Cross-cutting decisions baked into the sequence

**D1. "Stability bar" expanded beyond the design's narrow definition.** Per Scott's redirect c8, M1 absorbs in-flight non-MVP-critical work AND immediate accelerators alongside the original stability-bar set (bug fixes + instruction-contradictions). Rationale: a "thin pre-MVP stabilization" milestone would have left in-flight work and dogfooding-velocity wins floating without a milestone home. M1's broader frame respects the architect's daily-dogfooding bar as the actual stability bar.

**D2. "Immediate accelerators" introduced as a first-class roadmap category.** Per Scott's redirect c2, beads whose direct impact on daily dogfooding velocity is high — irrespective of vision-commitment alignment — are surfaced as a category, not buried in hygiene. The category is finite (M1-only); after M1 ships, "immediate accelerator" no longer applies as a sorting concept. The selected M1 set (nbrd, 19n9, e2l, clz, f298) is a synthesis-side judgment open to your refinement (Section 9, Q1).

**D3. gmxo is MVP; td39 is post-MVP.** Per Scott's redirect c5, the merge-gate redesign substrate ships in MVP (M3); the risk-tiered auto-merge policy ships post-MVP. Rationale: gmxo is a structural primitive; td39 is a policy applied on top. Shipping td39 without dogfooding gmxo first means tier definitions are guesswork. Post-MVP td39 lets a few weeks of MVP dogfooding inform which PR classes are genuinely low-risk.

**D4. 7bk.11 closes M4, not opens it.** Per Scott's redirect c6, the production shell driver wraps everything once worker-fleet mechanics are reliable. M4 is sequenced so the driver lands LAST in MVP — every other M4 capability (instrumentation, memory governance) is upstream of the driver hardening pass.

**D5. The off-thesis cut list is reframed as research spikes.** Per Scott's redirect c1, the 5-bead "cut" set (l0v0, nbrd, 7bk.24, zetc, cx6) is treated as a research-spike cluster: each bead's outcome is either retire (won't-do) or rescope (new value-add bead) — except nbrd, which is reclassified as an immediate accelerator. Section 6 of this doc handles each individually with explicit retire-or-rescope verdict.

**D6. Vision-alignment is the IN/OUT signal; structural critique is the ORDER signal; cluster intent is the HEADLINE signal.** When forks disagree, this is the synthesis precedence. Vision tag determines whether a bead lands in any milestone; structural deps determine which milestone; cluster theme determines the user-capability-headline framing.

**D7. Beadification is OUT of scope for this doc.** Section 8 lists structural changes recommended (parents, edges, labels), but executing them is Phase 5b with its own gating. This doc creates no beads, modifies no parents, applies no labels.

## 9. Open questions for Scott

(Q1, Q2, Q3 resolved at round-2 review — see Review history at top.)
(Q4 resolved at round-3 review — **C2 chosen**: pure parent-as-milestone with cleavage authorized. See Section 8 for the cleavage plan. Reasoning preserved below for reference.)

**Q4 (RESOLVED → C2). How should milestones be modeled in the bead data?**

The roadmap doc is the source of truth for milestone composition, but the bead system needs a queryable representation so we can answer: "is M1 done?" "what's in M2?" "where does new M3 work get inserted?"

**Options considered:**

| option | shape | pros | cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A. Milestone = parent epic** | Each M1-M4 is a top-level epic; in-scope beads reparented under it | Direct hierarchy in bd UI; queries trivial via `bd list --parent=M-id` | Beads have only one parent — would destroy existing 7bk / jyb / brainstorm-readiness epic semantics; or force milestones to nest inside epics, which inverts intended structure |
| **B. Milestone = label only** | Each in-scope bead carries `milestone-M<N>`; no tracking bead | Additive; doesn't disturb parent structure; trivial to add/remove | No place to store milestone-level DoD or user-capability headline; bd UI doesn't show milestone hierarchy |
| **C (round-2 recommendation, superseded by C2). Tracking-epic + label** | Per-milestone "tracking epic" bead holds the user-capability headline + DoD as AC; in-scope beads carry `milestone-M<N>` label additively (NOT reparented under the tracking epic) | Preserves existing parent semantics; queryable via labels; tracking bead is the docs home for milestone DoD | Two artifacts (label + tracking bead) need to stay aligned — but drift is recoverable from the roadmap doc |
| **C2 (chosen at round 3). Parent-as-milestone with cleavage** | Each milestone is a top-level feature bead. In-scope beads are reparented under their milestone (or an intermediate domain epic). Heterogeneous epics whose children span milestones (7bk, acmh) are cleaved: milestone-foreign children reparented out, then the epic itself rescoped to its residual role under its dominant milestone. | Single source of truth (the parent chain); no label/parent drift; `bd ready` natively reflects milestone gating via dep chain | One-time cost: cleaving heterogeneous epics requires a documented restructuring pass (Phase 5b plan) and a snapshot fail-safe |
| **D. External milestones manifest** | YAML/TOML manifest in repo lists milestone composition; no bd changes | Decouples from bd entirely | Two systems guaranteed to drift; bd users can't query for milestone state from `bd list` |

**Resolution: C2 — pure parent-as-milestone with cleavage authorized.** (Round-3 decision. Option C — tracking-epic + label hybrid — was the round-2 recommendation; superseded.)

**Why C2 over C:** Option C above preserved the existing parent slot for domain semantics (7bk = "specialized agent fleet epic," jyb = "universal flattening epic," etc.) and used labels for milestone tagging. C2 instead authorizes cleaving the heterogeneous epics whose children span multiple milestones (7bk, acmh) and re-anchors them under their dominant milestone, with redistributed children moved to their proper milestone parents. The decision: those monster epics evolved organically rather than by design; the milestone restructuring is the corrective surgery. The pre-restructure `issues.backup.jsonl` snapshot is the fail-safe.

**Mechanics (C2):**
- 6 top-level **feature** beads (`type=feature`): M1, M2, M3, M4, Post-MVP, Research-spikes — one per milestone/bucket. Description copied from this doc's milestone section; `acceptance` field set to the milestone DoD bullets.
- Milestone features have `depends-on` edges between themselves (M2 → M1, M3 → M2, M4 → M3, Post-MVP → M4) so `bd ready` reflects sequencing. Research-spikes is intentionally outside the chain (opportunistic).
- In-scope strategic beads are **reparented under** their milestone feature (or an intermediate domain epic, e.g., 7bk-fleet under M3, brainstorm-readiness epic under M2). Heterogeneous epics 7bk and acmh are rescoped (title/description/AC) to their residual M3-/M1-only roles after their milestone-foreign children are reparented OUT.
- `vision-85-5-10` label remains the cross-cutting tag for vision-advancing work; no new milestone-* labels are introduced under C2 (the parent chain encodes the milestone instead).

**Querying patterns (C2):**
- Is M1 done? → `bd show <M1-id>` reports status; or `bd list --parent <M1-id> --status open --status in_progress --json | jq length` returns 0 → close M1.
- What's in M2? → `bd list --parent <M2-id> --json` (direct) or walk the subtree via repeated `--parent` queries for nested epics.
- Where does new M3 work go? → file the bead with parent set to the appropriate domain epic (`7bk` for fleet concerns) or directly to the M3 feature for cross-cutting work.
- What's the M2 DoD? → `bd show <M2-id>` (the milestone feature's `acceptance_criteria`).
- Cross-milestone view? → traverse the milestone feature's subtree.
